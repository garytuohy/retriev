# Retriev Brand Guidelines

## Brand Positioning

**What we do:** Automatically recover failed Stripe payments for SaaS companies.

**For whom:** SaaS founders, CTOs, and finance leads who are losing 10-15% of MRR to failed payments.

**Why it matters:** Failed payments are invisible churn. Retriev makes it visible — and recovers it.

---

## Brand Personality

**We are:**
- **Direct** — We don't fluff. We say what we mean.
- **Competent** — We know payments. We know data.
- **Calm** — We're the steady hand recovering revenue.
- **Results-focused** — We measure success in dollars recovered.

**We are NOT:**
- Excitable or hype-driven
- Playful or casual
- Using emojis as communication
- Making claims we can't back with data

---

## Tone of Voice

### Headlines
- Short, declarative statements
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature
- No punctuation unless a question

**Examples:**
- "Recover failed payments automatically"
- "Stop losing revenue to card declines"
- "10-15% of your MRR is disappearing"

### Body Copy
- Second person ("you", "your")
- Active voice
- Specific numbers over vague claims
- One idea per paragraph
- No exclamation marks

**Examples:**
- "Retriev monitors your Stripe account for failed payments and triggers smart retry sequences."
- "Our AI identifies the optimal retry window for each customer based on their payment history."
- "Founders using Retriev recover an average of $2,800/month within 30 days."

### CTAs
- Action verbs
- No urgency words ("now", "today", "instantly")
- Clear next step

**Examples:**
- "Start recovering payments"
- "Connect your Stripe account"
- "Calculate your recovery potential"

## Visual Language

### Principles
1. **Clarity over cleverness** — If it confuses, cut it.
2. **Data as design** — Numbers are visual elements.
3. **Breathing room** — White space is content.
4. **One accent color** — Indigo is the brand. Everything else is neutral.

### Color System

```
Primary Background:   #09090b (near black)
Secondary Background: #18181b (card bg)
Tertiary Background:  #27272a (hover states)

Text Primary:         #fafafa (white)
Text Secondary:       #a1a1aa (muted)
Text Tertiary:        #71717a (labels)

Brand Primary:        #6366f1 (indigo)
Brand Hover:          #4f46e5 (darker indigo)
Brand Subtle:         rgba(99, 102, 241, 0.1) (backgrounds)

Success:              #22c55e (green - for positive metrics)
Warning:              #eab308 (yellow - for attention)
Error:                #ef4444 (red - for failures)

Border:               #27272a
Border Hover:         #3f3f46
```

### Typography

```
Font Family: Inter (weight 400-700)
Headings: Tight tracking (-0.02em), heavy weight (700-800)
Body: Normal tracking, regular weight (400-500)
Data/Metrics: Tabular numbers, medium weight (500-600)
```

### Iconography

**Style:**
- 24x24px default size
- 1.5px stroke weight
- Rounded caps and joins
- Single color (inherit from parent)

**Icon Set (Lucide-style):**
- Navigation: chevron-right, menu, x
- Actions: arrow-right, external-link, copy, check
- Status: check-circle, alert-circle, x-circle
- Features: zap (smart timing), mail (emails), chart-line (analytics), shield (security)
- Payment: credit-card, dollar-sign, receipt

**No emojis in UI. Ever.**

### Spacing Scale

```
4px   - Micro (icon gaps, inline elements)
8px   - Small (label padding, tight groups)
16px  - Medium (paragraph gaps, card padding)
24px  - Large (section padding, major gaps)
48px  - XL (section separators)
72px  - XXL (page sections)
```

### Border Radius

```
4px   - Buttons, tags, small elements
8px   - Cards, inputs, dropdowns
12px  - Large cards, modals
```

## What We Avoid

- ❌ Emojis in any UI or copy
- ❌ Exclamation marks
- ❌ Superlatives ("best", "amazing", "incredible")
- ❌ Vague claims without data
- ❌ Stock photo aesthetics
- ❌ Playful language or puns
- ❌ Multiple accent colors
- ❌ Gradient text (except logo)
- ❌ Rounded playful fonts

---

## Writing the Numbers

- Use commas in numbers: $12,450 not $12450
- Spell out "percent" in body copy, use "%" in UI
- Always include timeframe: "$2,800/month" not just "$2,800"
- Recovery rates: "73% average" not "up to 73%"
- MRR loss: "10-15% of MRR" not "up to 15%"

---
